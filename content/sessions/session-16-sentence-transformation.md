# Session 16: Sentence Transformation

## Introduction

Sentence transformation means changing the form of a sentence without changing the meaning. The golden rule is: meaning must remain the same.

## Transformation Types

Affirmative to negative:
He is honest.
He is not dishonest.

Assertive to interrogative:
Everyone loves success.
Who does not love success?

Assertive to exclamatory:
The view is very beautiful.
How beautiful the view is!

Simple to compound:
Getting up early, he went out.
He got up early and went out.

Simple to complex:
Being tired, she slept.
Because she was tired, she slept.

Compound to complex:
He is rich but unhappy.
Though he is rich, he is unhappy.

## Sentence Types

A simple sentence has one main clause.
Example: He went home.

A compound sentence has two main clauses joined by and, but, or, so.
Example: He was tired, but he worked.

A complex sentence has one main clause and one subordinate clause.
Example: Although he was tired, he worked.

Degree transformation:

Ravi is taller than Mohan.
Mohan is not as tall as Ravi.

## Practice

1. Change to negative: She is very intelligent.
2. Change to complex: Being tired, he slept early.
3. Change to interrogative: Everyone wants success.
4. Change to exclamatory: It is a wonderful day.

Suggested answers:

1. She is not unintelligent.
2. Because he was tired, he slept early.
3. Who does not want success?
4. What a wonderful day it is!
