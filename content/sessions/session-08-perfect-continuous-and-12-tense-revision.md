# Session 8: Perfect Continuous And 12 Tense Revision

## Introduction

Today we complete all 12 tenses. Perfect continuous tenses show an action plus its duration. They answer: for how long?

## Perfect Continuous Tenses

Present perfect continuous: has/have been + verb-ing.
I have been studying for two hours.
She has been learning English since April.

Past perfect continuous: had been + verb-ing.
They had been waiting for an hour before the bus arrived.

Future perfect continuous: will have been + verb-ing.
By June, I will have been learning English for one year.

Signals:

- for
- since
- how long
- by

## Perfect Vs Perfect Continuous

Use perfect when result matters.
I have written three letters. The result is three completed letters.

Use perfect continuous when duration matters.
I have been writing letters all morning. The activity and time are important.

Common Mistakes:

Wrong: I am studying since morning.
Correct: I have been studying since morning.

Wrong: She has been knowing him for years.
Correct: She has known him for years.

## 12 Tense Map

There are 3 times: present, past, future.
There are 4 aspects: simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous.
3 x 4 = 12 tenses.

Use one verb: write.

- I write.
- I am writing.
- I have written.
- I have been writing.
- I wrote.
- I was writing.
- I had written.
- I had been writing.
- I will write.
- I will be writing.
- I will have written.
- I will have been writing.
