# Session 11: Reported Speech - Statements

## Introduction

Reported speech means telling someone what another person said, without using the exact words.

## Direct To Indirect

Direct: Ravi said, 'I am hungry.'
Indirect: Ravi said that he was hungry.

Three things change:

1. Pronouns
2. Tense
3. Time and place words

Pronouns:

I changes according to the speaker. You changes according to the listener.

Tense backshift:

- am/is/are -> was/were
- present simple -> past simple
- past simple -> past perfect
- has/have -> had
- will -> would
- can -> could
- may -> might

Time words:

- now -> then
- today -> that day
- tomorrow -> the next day
- yesterday -> the previous day
- here -> there
- this -> that

## Examples

She said, "I love my school.
She said that she loved her school.

He said, "I am studying now.
He said that he was studying then.

Ravi said, "I will call you tomorrow.
Ravi said that he would call me the next day.

Mother said, "I have cooked dinner.
Mother said that she had cooked dinner.

Teacher said, "Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
Teacher said that water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

Universal truths do not change.

## Common Mistakes

Wrong: He said that he will come tomorrow.
Correct: He said that he would come the next day.

Wrong: She said that I am tired.
Correct: She said that she was tired.

## Practice

Convert:

1. Priya said, "I am reading a novel.
2. The guard said, "The gate closes at 10 p.m.
3. Rohan said to me, "I saw your brother yesterday.

Answers:

1. Priya said that she was reading a novel.
2. The guard said that the gate closed at 10 p.m.
3. Rohan told me that he had seen my brother the previous day.

Copy a 100-word passage in cursive. Focus on flow and neatness.

## Speaking Practice

News reading:

Read like a newsreader: slow, clear, calm, with pauses.
