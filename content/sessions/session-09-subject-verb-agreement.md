# Session 9: Subject-Verb Agreement

## Introduction

Today we learn concord, which means agreement. The subject and verb must match.

## Basic Agreement

Singular subject takes singular verb.
The boy plays.
The girl sings.

Plural subject takes plural verb.
The boys play.
The girls sing.

In simple present, singular verbs often end with s, but plural verbs do not.

Explain:

The s jumps. One boy plays. Many boys play.

## Important Rules

Two subjects joined by and usually take plural verb:
Ravi and Seema are here.

But one idea takes singular:
Bread and butter is my breakfast.

With either...or and neither...nor, the verb agrees with the nearer subject:
Neither the captain nor the players are ready.
Neither the players nor the captain is ready.

Each, every, everyone, somebody, nobody take singular verb:
Everyone is invited.
Each of the boys is present.

One of the + plural noun takes singular verb:
One of the books is missing.

Some nouns look plural but are singular:
news, mathematics, physics.

Some nouns look singular but are plural:
police, cattle.

Paired items are plural:
scissors, trousers, spectacles.

## Common Mistakes

Wrong: Each of the boys are present.
Correct: Each of the boys is present.

Wrong: My trousers is new.
Correct: My trousers are new.

Wrong: The box of chocolates are on the table.
Correct: The box of chocolates is on the table.

Ignore the words in between. The subject is box, not chocolates.

## Practice

1. One of my friends ___ in Canada. (live)
2. Physics ___ difficult. (be)
3. The box of pens ___ on the shelf. (is/are)
4. Correct: The furniture are old.

Answers:

1. lives
2. is
3. is
4. The furniture is old.
