# Session 13: Modals

## Introduction

Today we study modals. Modals are helping verbs that add meaning like ability, permission, possibility, advice, request, or obligation.

## Modal Meanings

Can shows ability or informal permission:
She can swim. Can I borrow your pen?

Could shows past ability or polite request:
He could run fast as a child. Could you help me?

May shows formal permission or possibility:
May I come in? It may rain.

Might shows weak possibility:
He might be at the library.

Must shows strong obligation or strong guess:
You must wear a helmet. She must be tired.

Should shows advice:
You should revise daily.

Would shows polite request or imagined situation:
Would you like tea? I would travel if I had money.

## Modal Grammar Rule

After a modal, always use base verb.

Correct: He can swim.
Wrong: He can to swim.

Correct: You must attend.
Wrong: You must to attend.

Correct: I would like to join.
Wrong: I would liked to join.

Certainty ladder:

- must = almost sure
- should = likely
- may/might/could = possible

Permission:

In exams and formal situations, use may for permission.

## Practice

1. ___ you please explain this again? (polite request)
2. You ___ wear a seatbelt. It is the law.
3. Correct: He can to swim.
4. Correct: When she was young, she can speak French.

Answers:

1. Could/Would
2. must
3. He can swim.
4. When she was young, she could speak French.
