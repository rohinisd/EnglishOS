# Session 7: Continuous And Perfect Tenses

## Introduction

Last session we studied simple tenses. Today we learn continuous tenses and perfect tenses. Continuous shows ongoing action. Perfect shows completed action connected to another time.

## Continuous Tenses

Present continuous: am/is/are + verb-ing.
I am reading. She is writing. They are playing.
Use for actions happening now.

Past continuous: was/were + verb-ing.
I was reading. They were playing.
Use for actions going on at a past time.

Future continuous: will be + verb-ing.
I will be studying at 8 p.m.

Signals:

- now, at this moment, currently
- while, as, at 8 o'clock yesterday
- at this time tomorrow

Common Mistake:

Wrong: I am knowing the answer.
Correct: I know the answer.

Know, believe, love, understand, and want are stative verbs. They are usually not used in continuous form.

## Perfect Tenses

Present perfect: has/have + V3.
I have finished my homework.
She has gone to the market.

Past perfect: had + V3.
The train had left before we arrived.

Future perfect: will have + V3.
By next year, I will have completed my course.

Teach V1, V2, V3:

go, went, gone
eat, ate, eaten
write, wrote, written
see, saw, seen

Since vs for:

Since gives starting point: since Monday, since 2020.
For gives duration: for two hours, for ten years.

## Practice

1. Change: She writes a letter. -> present continuous.
2. Fill: I ___ three movies this week. (see)
3. Identify: The movie had started before we arrived.
4. Correct: He has left yesterday.
5. Fill: They ___ for the bus when it rained. (wait)

Answers:

1. She is writing a letter.
2. have seen
3. past perfect
4. He left yesterday.
5. were waiting
