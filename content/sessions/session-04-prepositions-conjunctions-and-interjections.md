# Session 4: Prepositions, Conjunctions, And Interjections

## Introduction

Today we learn small words that do big work. Prepositions show relation, conjunctions join ideas, and interjections show emotion.

## Prepositions

A preposition shows the relation between two things. It may show place, time, or direction.

Place: in, on, under, beside, between, behind.
Time: at, on, in, before, after, during, since, for.
Direction: to, into, over, across, through.

The book is on the table.
The cat is under the chair.
She arrived at 5 p.m.
We met on Monday.
He was born in 2010.

Time rule:

Use at for exact time: at 5 p.m.
Use on for days and dates: on Monday, on 5 May.
Use in for months, years, and long periods: in June, in 2026, in the morning.

Fixed phrases:

good at, afraid of, interested in, depend on, proud of, married to.

## Conjunctions

A conjunction joins words, phrases, or sentences.

I like tea and coffee.
I studied hard but I was nervous.
We stayed home because it was raining.

Coordinating conjunctions join equal parts. Remember FANBOYS: for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so.

Subordinating conjunctions introduce dependent ideas: because, although, if, when, while, since, until.

Correlative conjunctions work in pairs:
either...or
neither...nor
not only...but also
both...and

Common Mistake:

Wrong: Neither Ravi or Seema came.
Correct: Neither Ravi nor Seema came.

## Interjections

An interjection shows sudden emotion.

Wow! That is beautiful.
Alas! He failed.
Hurray! We won.
Ouch! That hurt.

Use interjections carefully in formal writing. They are common in stories and dialogues.

## Practice

1. Fill: She is good ___ drawing.
2. Fill: I was born ___ 2010.
3. Join: It was raining. We went out.
4. Complete: Neither Riya ___ Priya came.
5. Find interjection: Wow! What a goal!

Answers: at, in, although/but, nor, Wow.

## Cursive Practice

Loop letters: l, b, h, k, e, f. Keep loops slim and tall. Practise ll, bb, hh.

## Speaking Practice

Read aloud a paragraph. Focus on pauses at commas and full stops.

Peer feedback:

- Was the speed comfortable?
- Were words clear?
- Did the reader pause properly?

## Writing Practice

An informal letter is friendly and personal. Write to a friend or cousin.

Homework: Write a letter to a cousin describing your summer trip.
