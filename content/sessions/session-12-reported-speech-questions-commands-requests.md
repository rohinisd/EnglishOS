# Session 12: Reported Speech - Questions, Commands, Requests

## Introduction

Last session we reported statements. Today we report questions, commands, requests, advice, and exclamations.

## Questions

Yes/no questions use asked if or asked whether.

Direct: He said, 'Are you coming?'
Indirect: He asked if I was coming.

WH questions keep the WH word:
Direct: She said, 'Where do you live?'
Indirect: She asked where I lived.

Important: reported questions do not use question word order.

Wrong: He asked where do I live.
Correct: He asked where I lived.

## Commands, Requests, Advice

Commands use told or ordered + to + V1.

The officer said, 'Stop.'
The officer ordered me to stop.

Requests use requested + object + to + V1.

She said, 'Please help me.'
She requested me to help her.

Advice uses advised + object + to + V1.

Mother said, 'Drink warm water.'
Mother advised me to drink warm water.

Negative commands use not to + V1.

He said, 'Do not shout.'
He told me not to shout.

## Exclamations

Exclamations become statements with exclaimed joyfully, exclaimed sorrowfully, or exclaimed with surprise.

Direct: 'Hurray! We won,' they said.
Indirect: They exclaimed joyfully that they had won.

Direct: 'Alas! I failed,' he said.
Indirect: He exclaimed sorrowfully that he had failed.

## Practice

Convert:

1. He said, "Are you happy?
2. Mother said, "Close the window.
3. The teacher said, "How old are you?
4. Priya said, "Please wait for me.

Answers:

1. He asked if I was happy.
2. Mother told me to close the window.
3. The teacher asked how old I was.
4. Priya requested me to wait for her.
