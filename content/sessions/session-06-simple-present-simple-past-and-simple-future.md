# Session 6: Simple Present, Simple Past, And Simple Future

## Introduction

Today we begin tenses. Tense tells us time: now, before, or later. The simple tenses are the foundation.

## Simple Present

Simple present is used for habits, facts, routines, and universal truths.

I play cricket every day.
She plays piano.
The sun rises in the east.

With I, you, we, they, use base verb: I go, they play.
With he, she, it, add s or es: he goes, she plays, it works.

Signal words: daily, every day, always, usually, often, sometimes, never.

Common Mistake:

Wrong: She go to school.
Correct: She goes to school.

## Simple Past

Simple past is used for completed actions in the past.

I visited my grandmother yesterday.
We played football last week.
Gandhi led the Dandi March in 1930.

Regular verbs add ed: walk, walked; play, played.
Irregular verbs change: go, went; eat, ate; see, saw.

Signal words: yesterday, last week, ago, in 2015, when I was young.

Common Mistake:

Wrong: I goed to the market.
Correct: I went to the market.

## Simple Future

Simple future uses will plus base verb.

I will call you tomorrow.
She will complete homework.
They will visit us next week.

After will, do not use to.

Wrong: I will to call.
Correct: I will call.

Signal words: tomorrow, next week, soon, later, in a few days.

## Practice

1. My brother ___ to college every morning. (go)
2. Change to past: She writes a letter.
3. Change to future: They play cricket.
4. Identify tense: The Ganga flows through Varanasi.
5. Correct: He don't likes spicy food.

Answers:

1. goes
2. She wrote a letter.
3. They will play cricket.
4. simple present
5. He does not like spicy food.
