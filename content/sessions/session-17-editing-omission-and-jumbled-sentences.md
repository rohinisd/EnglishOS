# Session 17: Editing, Omission, And Jumbled Sentences

## Introduction

Today is exam technique day. Editing, omission, and jumbled sentences are scoring areas if you know the method.

## Editing

In editing, each line has one error. Common errors:

- tense
- subject-verb agreement
- preposition
- article
- pronoun
- spelling

Read the full sentence first. Then check slowly.

Examples:

She go to school. -> She goes to school.
My sister don't like tea. -> My sister doesn't like tea.
The book on the table are mine. -> The book on the table is mine.

## Omission

In omission, a word is missing. Missing words are usually small: a, an, the, is, are, of, to, and, but.

Example:
He is going ^ market.
Missing: to/the
Correct: He is going to the market.

He is one ^ best students.
Missing: of/the
Correct: He is one of the best students.

## Jumbled Sentences

Use the S-V-O-P-T skeleton:
Subject + Verb + Object + Place + Time.

Words: won / the / yesterday / we / match
Sentence: We won the match yesterday.

Find the subject first, then verb, then object, then details.

## Practice

1. Edit: My sister don't like spicy food.
2. Insert: He is one ^ best students in class.
3. Rearrange: go / morning / every / I / for / a / walk
4. Edit: The book on the table are mine.

Answers:

1. My sister doesn't like spicy food.
2. of the
3. I go for a walk every morning.
4. The book on the table is mine.
