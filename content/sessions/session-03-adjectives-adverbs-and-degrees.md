# Session 3: Adjectives, Adverbs, And Degrees

## Introduction

Today we will learn how to add colour and movement to sentences. Nouns and verbs are the base, but adjectives and adverbs make the sentence clearer and more interesting.

## Adjectives

An adjective describes a noun. It tells us what kind, how many, or which one.

What kind: a red rose, an honest boy, a clever student.
How many: five books, three apples.
Which one: this bag, that chair.

Without adjective: I saw a dog.
With adjective: I saw a small brown dog.

The second sentence gives a clearer picture.

Think About It:

Find adjectives: The tall girl carried a heavy black bag.

Answer: tall, heavy, black.

## Adverbs

An adverb describes a verb. It tells how, when, where, or how often an action happens.

How: She sings sweetly.
When: He came yesterday.
Where: They waited outside.
How often: I always revise my notes.

Many adverbs end in ly: slowly, quickly, softly, carefully. But not all. Fast is already an adverb. We do not say fastly.

Common Mistake:

Wrong: He runs fastly.
Correct: He runs fast.

## Degrees Of Comparison

Adjectives have three degrees.

Positive: tall
Comparative: taller
Superlative: tallest

Positive simply describes. Comparative compares two. Superlative compares more than two.

Short adjectives usually take er and est: small, smaller, smallest.
Long adjectives use more and most: beautiful, more beautiful, most beautiful.

Irregular forms:
good, better, best
bad, worse, worst
little, less, least
many/much, more, most

Common Mistakes:

Wrong: more taller
Correct: taller

Wrong: most best
Correct: best

## Practice

1. Find adjective and adverb: The clever boy quickly solved the puzzle.
2. Give degrees of intelligent.
3. Correct: My school is more bigger than yours.
4. Fill: Of the two sisters, Meera is the taller/tallest.
5. Arrange: a green big old car.

Answers:

1. clever = adjective, quickly = adverb
2. intelligent, more intelligent, most intelligent
3. My school is bigger than yours.
4. taller
5. a big old green car

## Cursive Practice

Peak letters: i, u, t, s, p, r. Keep the small peaks smooth and even. The letter s should be a curve, not a zigzag.

## Speaking Practice

Show and tell:

Choose one object. Speak for 90 seconds. Use at least 3 adjectives and 2 adverbs.

Model:

This is my old blue diary. I received it from my sister. I write in it carefully every night.

## Writing Practice

Notice writing:

A notice gives important information to a group. It must answer what, when, where, who, and contact.

Homework: Write a notice for an inter-house debate competition.
