# Session 5: Articles And Determiners

## Introduction

Today we study tiny words that change meaning: a, an, the, some, many, much, few, little. These words come before nouns and help us understand whether something is general, specific, countable, or uncountable.

## Articles

A, an, and the are articles.

Use a before a consonant sound: a boy, a cat, a university. University begins with u but sounds like you, so we say a university.

Use an before a vowel sound: an apple, an orange, an hour, an honest man. Hour and honest begin with h, but h is silent.

Use the when we are talking about something specific.

I saw a dog. The dog was barking.
First mention: a dog. Second mention: the dog.

Use the for unique things: the sun, the moon, the earth.
Use the before rivers, oceans, mountain ranges: the Ganga, the Indian Ocean, the Himalayas.
Use the before superlatives: the tallest, the best.

Zero article:

Do not use the before most country names, languages, and subjects.

India is beautiful.
I speak Hindi.
Mathematics is difficult.

Wrong: the India
Correct: India

## Determiners

Determiners come before nouns and tell how much, how many, which one, or whose.

Examples: this, that, these, those, my, your, some, any, many, much, few, little, each, every.

Use many with countable nouns: many books, many students.
Use much with uncountable nouns: much water, much money.

Use few with countable nouns and little with uncountable nouns.

A few means some and sounds positive.
Few means almost none and sounds negative.

I have a few friends. That means I have some friends.
I have few friends. That means almost no friends.

A little means some.
Little means almost none.

## Common Mistakes

Wrong: He is a honest man.
Correct: He is an honest man.

Wrong: Delhi is the capital of the India.
Correct: Delhi is the capital of India.

Wrong: I have much books.
Correct: I have many books.

Wrong: He is best player.
Correct: He is the best player.

## Practice

1. ___ MBA from ___ IIM is ___ dream.
2. I have ___ questions to ask. (few/a few)
3. ___ French is ___ beautiful language.
4. There is ___ hope left, so do not give up. (little/a little)

Answers:

1. An MBA from an IIM is a dream.
2. a few
3. French is a beautiful language.
4. a little

## Writing Practice

Formal letter:

A formal letter is polite, official, and direct. It has sender address, date, receiver address, subject, salutation, body, and closing.

Homework: Write a formal letter to the principal requesting permission for a school event.
