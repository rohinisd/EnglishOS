# Session 10: Active And Passive Voice

## Introduction

Voice tells us what the sentence focuses on: the doer or the receiver. Active voice focuses on the doer. Passive voice focuses on the action or receiver.

## Active Vs Passive

Active: Meera broke the window.
Passive: The window was broken by Meera.

Active pattern: Subject + Verb + Object.
Passive pattern: Object + be verb + V3 + by + Subject.

Steps:

1. Find subject, verb, object.
2. Move object to the front.
3. Add correct be verb.
4. Change main verb to V3.
5. Add by plus doer if needed.

## Passive Forms

Simple present:
Active: The chef cooks meals.
Passive: Meals are cooked by the chef.

Simple past:
Active: Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.
Passive: Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.

Present perfect:
Active: Someone has stolen my bicycle.
Passive: My bicycle has been stolen.

Continuous:
Active: The teacher is explaining the chapter.
Passive: The chapter is being explained by the teacher.

Modal:
Active: You must obey the rules.
Passive: The rules must be obeyed.

Drop by phrase when doer is unknown, general, or obvious.

## Common Mistakes

Wrong: The cake was baked from my mother.
Correct: The cake was baked by my mother.

Wrong: The homework has completed.
Correct: The homework has been completed.

Wrong: Rice is eaten in India by the people.
Correct: Rice is eaten in India.

## Practice

1. Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.
2. The window has been broken by the children. Change to active.
3. Tea ___ every morning. (make, passive)
4. They are building a new bridge.

Answers:

1. Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.
2. The children have broken the window.
3. is made
4. A new bridge is being built by them.

## Cursive Practice

Copy a 10-word sentence five times. First neat, then faster. Target: under 60 seconds.

## Speaking Practice

Job interview role play.

Questions:

- Tell me about yourself.
- Why do you want this job?
- What are your strengths?
- What is one weakness?

Use passive:

- I was selected as class monitor.
- I have been trained in basic computer skills.
