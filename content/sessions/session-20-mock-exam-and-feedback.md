# Session 20: Mock Exam And Feedback

## Introduction

Today is the final session. This is not just a test. It is a chance to see how far you have come in grammar, writing, handwriting, and speaking.

## Exam Pattern

A full English paper usually has three sections:

Section A: Reading
Unseen passage, MCQs, short answers, vocabulary.

Section B: Writing and Grammar
Letter, email, article, report, editing, omission, transformation, gap filling.

Section C: Literature
Extract questions, short answers, long answers.

Time plan:

- Reading: 35 minutes
- Writing and Grammar: 55 minutes
- Literature: 75 minutes
- Revision: 15 minutes

## Exam Strategy

Read every question twice. Underline command words like explain, describe, compare, justify.

For long writing, plan for 2 to 3 minutes before writing. A plan saves time because you do not get stuck halfway.

Attempt questions you know well first. Keep handwriting neat. Leave one line between answers. Do not spend too much time on one question.

## Writing And Grammar Drill

Practice Set:

- one editing set
- one transformation set
- one gap-filling tense set
- one punctuation set

Use fast correction:

Find subject first. Check tense signal words. Check articles. Check prepositions. Check punctuation.

## Mock Writing Task

Choose one:

Option A: Formal letter to the manager of an online store about a defective product.

Option B: Article on "Plastic Pollution: A Silent Killer".

Planning steps:

1. Decide format.
2. Write 3 main points.
3. Decide closing line.
4. Write neatly.

Rubric:

- format
- content
- grammar
- vocabulary
- neatness

## Final Cursive Evaluation

Compare today's handwriting with Session 1. Check slant, spacing, letter size, and overall cleanness.

## Final Speaking

Elocution:

Topic: My Journey Through This Programme.

Structure:

- Where I started
- What I learned
- What improved
- What I will continue practising

## Closing

You have completed 20 sessions covering grammar, cursive, writing, and speaking. The goal was not only marks. The goal was confidence. Continue reading daily, writing weekly, and speaking whenever you get a chance.
