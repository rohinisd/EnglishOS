# Session 19: Punctuation And Full Revision Drill

## Introduction

Punctuation marks are traffic signals for writing. Without them, sentences become confusing.

## Punctuation Marks

Full stop ends a statement:
I finished my work.

Question mark ends a question:
Where are you going?

Exclamation mark shows strong feeling:
What a beautiful day!

Comma separates items or pauses:
We bought apples, oranges, and bananas.

Apostrophe shows contraction or possession:
It's raining. Riya's book.

Quotation marks show direct speech:
She said, 'I will come.'

Colon introduces a list:
He needs three things: patience, courage, and honesty.

Semicolon joins related independent clauses:
She studied hard; however, she failed.

## Capitalisation And Common Errors

Start every sentence with a capital letter. Capitalise proper nouns. Always capitalise I.

Wrong: its raining outside
Correct: It's raining outside.

Wrong: She asked, where are you going.
Correct: She asked, 'Where are you going?'

Wrong: My father, is a doctor.
Correct: My father is a doctor.

Do not put a comma between a simple subject and verb.

Its vs it's:

It's means it is. Its means belonging to it.
It's raining. The dog wagged its tail.

## Practice

Punctuate:

1. ravi said i will come tomorrow
2. shes going to mumbai next week
3. we bought bread butter milk and eggs
4. its a beautiful day isnt it

Answers:

1. Ravi said, "I will come tomorrow.
2. She's going to Mumbai next week.
3. We bought bread, butter, milk, and eggs.
4. It's a beautiful day, isn't it?

## Speaking Practice

Panel interview:

Questions:

- Tell us about yourself.
- What are your strengths?
- What is one weakness?
- What is your goal?
- What current issue do you care about?

Answer in 45 to 60 seconds.
