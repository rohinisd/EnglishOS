# Session 18: Vocabulary - Idioms, Phrasal Verbs, One-Word Substitution

## Introduction

Vocabulary makes language powerful. Today we learn three tools: idioms, phrasal verbs, and one-word substitutions.

## Idioms

An idiom is a phrase whose meaning is different from the literal words.

Piece of cake: very easy.
The exam was a piece of cake.

Spill the beans: reveal a secret.
Do not spill the beans about the surprise party.

Under the weather: unwell.
I am feeling under the weather.

Bite the bullet: face a difficult situation.
He had to bite the bullet and apologise.

Blessing in disguise: something bad that becomes good.
Missing the bus was a blessing in disguise because I met my old friend.

## Phrasal Verbs

A phrasal verb is verb plus particle.

Give up: quit.
Do not give up.

Look after: care for.
Please look after my dog.

Put off: postpone.
The meeting was put off.

Call off: cancel.
The match was called off.

Turn down: reject.
He turned down the offer.

Find out: discover.
I found out the truth.

Common Mistake:

Wrong: He gave up from smoking.
Correct: He gave up smoking.

## One-Word Substitution

One-word substitution replaces a long phrase with one precise word.

One who does not eat meat: vegetarian.
One who cannot read or write: illiterate.
A person who walks on foot: pedestrian.
A person who studies stars: astronomer.
Government by the people: democracy.
A place where books are kept: library.
A person who knows many languages: polyglot.
