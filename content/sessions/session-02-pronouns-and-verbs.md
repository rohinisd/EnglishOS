# Session 2: Pronouns And Verbs

## Introduction And Recap

In the last session, we learned that nouns are naming words. Today we will learn what happens when we do not want to repeat the noun again and again. That is where pronouns help. Then we will study verbs, the heartbeat of every sentence.

Recap questions:

- What is a noun?
- Give one proper noun.
- Give one abstract noun.

## Pronouns

A pronoun is a word used in place of a noun. Pronouns make sentences smooth.

Without pronouns: Priya opened Priya's bag and took Priya's book.
With pronouns: Priya opened her bag and took her book.

Subject pronouns do the action: I, you, he, she, it, we, they.
Object pronouns receive the action: me, him, her, us, them.

Example:
He called me.
He is the subject. Me is the object.

Possessive pronouns show ownership: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs.

Reflexive pronouns end in self or selves: myself, yourself, himself, herself, ourselves, themselves.

Use reflexive pronouns when the subject and object are the same person:
She hurt herself.
I taught myself.

Common Mistake:

Wrong: Me and Rohan are going.
Correct: Rohan and I are going.

Put yourself last and use I when you are the subject.

## Verbs

A verb is a doing or being word. Without a verb, a group of words is not a complete sentence.

Action verbs: run, eat, write, speak, jump.
Being verbs: is, am, are, was, were.
Thinking or feeling verbs: know, believe, love, understand.

Main verb shows the real action.
Helping verb supports the main verb.

Examples:
She is writing a letter.
Writing is the main verb. Is is the helping verb.

They have finished homework.
Finished is the main verb. Have is the helping verb.

Important helping verbs:

is, am, are, was, were, has, have, had, do, does, did, will, shall, can, could, may, might, must, should, would.

## Common Mistakes

Wrong: She do homework.
Correct: She does homework.

Wrong: I am knowing the answer.
Correct: I know the answer.

Wrong: Give the pen to Riya and I.
Correct: Give the pen to Riya and me.

Use the drop-the-other-person trick. Say: Give the pen to me. That sounds correct. So: Give the pen to Riya and me.

## Practice

Ask students:

1. Replace with pronouns: Ananya lost Ananya's pen.
2. Find helping and main verb: They have been waiting.
3. Choose: Between you and I/me, this is a secret.
4. Underline verbs: She has eaten lunch and is reading a book.

Answers:

1. Ananya lost her pen.
2. have been = helping, waiting = main
3. me
4. has eaten, is reading

## Cursive Practice

Today we practise wave letters: c, a, d, g, o, q. They all begin with a smooth undercurve. Keep them rounded, not pointed.

Task: 2 lines of c a d g o q, then write "a good cat" five times.

## Speaking Practice

Tongue twisters:

- She sells seashells
- Red lorry, yellow lorry
- Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers

Picture talk:

Look at a picture and describe it using pronouns: He is selling fruit. She is buying vegetables. They are standing near a shop.

## Writing Practice

Teach message writing:

A message is short. It gives important information when someone is absent.

Homework: Write a 50-word message from a phone call received for your mother.
